Denver Vue.js 2/26/18

People I Met

Hideyuki Tokuda - Software Developer at HomeAdvisor

CJ - Galvanize instructor

What I Learned

Vue is a really nice and clean framework for Javascript built from Angular. There are a lot of great features and it is completely open source and not funded or acquired by a large corporation, making it a very desirable platform for developers to work on. However, the lack of funding has also caused a slower adoption of the platform by large companies because it makes them weary. There is a very large following and there is a big push from the commnuity to start opening companies to the platform migrating towards using it.

Building Strava Product Experiences 3/1

People I Met

Steve Lloyd - Sr. Director of Product Engineering at Strava

Kathy Keating - CTO at Apostrophe, Inc.

What I Learned

Strava's product is more than a gps tracker for your bike rides. Their goal is to be the social network of athletes. They initially started with a product and release cycle that would take a quarter conduct an a/b test and send updates to their users. After redeisnging their product and coming up with a few operational strategies, they are now releasing an update once every week, acquiring more users and less churn.

This was by far the best meetup I attended for networking purposes. I also learned that in order for a product to stay relevant and be successful, it must constantly appeal to users and maintain the userbase. Coming from the operational side of a small tech company, this made me reflect on how our product cycles ran and what the company could have improved on.

Questions I Have

How will a company like Strava maintain it's standing in the marketplace as other fitness apps like FitBit are constantly growing and staying a step ahead? What will set them apart?

What are some resources to learn more about iOS app development?
